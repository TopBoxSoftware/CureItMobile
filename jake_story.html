Jake Samson

Some of the names and places featured in this interview may have been changed to protect the identities of those involved.
When and how did you first find out you had cancer?
In December of 2014 I was having very dull and mild pain on my left side, so like most people I hopped on the Internet and self diagnosed myself with a hernia. At the time I didn't have insurance so I had to wait until February to see my primary care doctor.

When I saw my doctor he felt around on my abdomen, felt my testicles and had me cough, he then told me I had a bacterial infection in my testicles called epididymitis and prescribed me anti-inflammatories and antibiotics.

Within 5 days my left testicle swelled up 3 times the size of my right. I called my doctor and he advised me to go to the ER. So I went to the ER and the doctor there also said it was a bacterial infection but he would do an ultra sound just to be sure.

About 4 hours later he came back into my room and started asking me if I had a family history of testicular cancer. My heart felt like it sank into my stomach. I told him no, he then proceeded to tell me the ultra sound had found a tumor on my left testicle and I would need to see a urologist first thing in the morning.

So I went and saw the urologist the next day and he told me it was indeed a tumor but in order to biopsy it they would need to remove the testicle, so they scheduled me for surgery one week later. This was all extremely difficult to process. I let my family know of the situation but stayed very optimistic that the tumor would be benign.

So I had surgery which was very uncomfortable for about 2 weeks. I needed help getting up and down from chairs and out of bed. About 3 weeks after the surgery I went back to the urologist and he seemed to be very up best so I'm thinking he's going to give me good news. Wrong. It was indeed cancer.

They way he explained it was I had the "good kind" which was called "seminoma" and could be treated by radiation. He then scheduled me for a CT scan before I went to see a radiation oncologist. I went for the CT scan later that week and followed up with the urologist the next day.

He informed me the cancer was stage 2 and had spread the the lymph nodes and I needed to see an oncologist. The next day I met with a radiation oncologist and he gave me even more bad news.

The initial biopsy was incorrect and the cancer I had was a rarer form of testicular cancer (non-seminoma) and is completely resistant I radiation. So he sent me down the hall to meet with a medical oncologist. He told me I would need to go through chemotherapy.
