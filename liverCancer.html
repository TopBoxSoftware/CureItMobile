 Introduction (1/3)
Liver Cancers are cancers that involve the growth and spread of abnormal cells in the liver.

There are several different types of Liver Cancer, all of which possess different characteristics and require a different approach in treatment.

The four that we will be taking a closer look at will be Hepatocellular Carcinomas, Bile Duct Cancers, Angiosarcomas and Hemangiosarcomas.
Introduction (2/3)
The liver is often noted as being one of the most important organs in the body.

It processes substances such as food and drink into valuable energy and nutrients that the body can store for later, and is involved with removing dangerous substances from blood.

Liver Cancer interrupts the normal functioning of the liver due to the growth of abnormal cells. Also worth noting is that a large portion of Liver Cancer deaths have been linked to lifestyle factors, something which means that prevention is possible many people.

Types of Cancer (1/2)
Hepatocellular Carcinomas
 Despite being strangely rare in the United States, Hepatocellular Carcinomas are by far the most common form of Liver Cancer.

In fact, they have risen in prominence in recent years to become the third leading cause of cancer deaths worldwide, with well over 500,000 people affected.

Types of Cancer (2/2)
Bile Duct Cancers
 Bile Duct Cancers are a rare form of cancer that starts in the thin tubes that reach from the liver to the small intestine. It is fairly uncommon in the United States, accounting for about only 2,000-3,000 of the 33,000 cases of Liver Cancer that occur there each year.

That said, it is much more common in parts of Southeast Asia due to the relative prominence of a parasitic infection that has been linked to causing Bile Duct Cancer.

Medical Advancements (1/2)
Advancements in Understanding
 One of the most powerful tools that has emerged in recent years to fight Liver Cancer is an increased understanding of what causes the disease in the first place.

This allows doctors and researchers alike to operate under a set of guidelines that facilitate the early detection and prevention of the disease.
