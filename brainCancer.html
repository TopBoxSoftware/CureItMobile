 Introduction (1/3)
Brain Cancer is a disease in which a group of cancerous cells begin developing in a person's brain tissue.

This can have massive implications on the functioning of typical brain operations such as muscle control, sensation, and memory development

All Brain Cancers fall into two primary categories: Primary and Secondary. However, there do exist other classifications of this deadly disease, such as Meningiomas and Glioblastomas. All of these terms are covered in greater detail down below.
Introduction (2/3)
There are nearly 700,000 people in the United States are currently living with Brain Cancer, with over 191 cases being discovered each and every day worldwide.

While the threat of Brain Cancer can impact anyone, it is statistically more common in younger children and older adults. Brain Cancer accounts for 25 percent of all cancers seen in children.

The side effects of Brain Cancer can persist throughout the remainder of a victim's life, thus necessitating further research to eliminate this unecessary threat.

Types of Cancer (1/2)
Primary/Secondary
 Evaluating whether a Brain Cancer is Primary or Secondary is an essential step in terms of developing an effective treatment plan.

Only about 20 percent of Brain Cancers are primary. This means that while primary brain tumors only account for around 2 percent of all cancer cases in adults, the reach of Brain Cancer indirectly harms thousands more through secondary brain tumors.

Types of Cancer (2/2)
Meningiomas
 Meningiomas are, simply put, tumors that arise from the coverings of the brain and spinal cord.

They typically are found on the outer curve and top of the brain. Meningiomas are fairly common, accounting for nearly a third of all Primary Brain Cancers.

Medical Advancements (1/2)
Virus Treatment
 The use of antiviral drugs has been a treatment method that has bore some success in recent years.

For instance, a 2006 clinical trial conducted by Dr. Cecilia Soderberg-Naucler made use of Valcyte - an antiviral drug usually used to treat viruses in AIDS patients - to combat some of the more aggressive forms of Brain Cancer.

Medical Advancements (2/2)
Laser Treatment
 One of the most valuable ways to approach an issue as large as Brain Cancer is to experiement with what may seem like crazy ideas.

That's exactly what Dr. Ricardo Komotar, chair of the Florida Neurosurgical Society did with his Visualase Thermal Therapy System, a laser based therapy designed to treat advanced forms of Brain Cancer with minimal side effects.

How to Help (1/2)
American Brain Tumor Association
 Founded over 40 years ago, The American Brain Tumor Association was the first nonprofit organization in the world to set its sights solely on eliminating the risk of Brain Tumors.

Every dollar donated to them is used to not only pursue breakthroughs in Brain Cancer diagnosis, but also to support the needs of both patients and those that look after them.

How to Help (2/2)
National Brain Tumor Society
 The National Brain Tumor Society has been searching tirelessly for potential breakthroughs in Brain Cancer research for well over 30 years now.

Their goal is simple: to provide hope for those currently afflicted with a form of Brain Cancer whilst also funding boundry-pushing research to ensure that this issue becomes one of the past.
