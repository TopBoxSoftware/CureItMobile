 Introduction (1/3)
Lymphoma is classified as the uncontrollable growth of a specific type of white blood cell called a lymphatic cell. It is a variant of Blood Cancer, but possesses enough unique properties to warrant individual analysis.

There are 2 main types of Lymphatic Cancers: Hodgkin's Disease, and Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma.

The specifics of both of these will be dissected in great detail down below.
Introduction (2/3)
Because Lymphoma affects the lymphatic system, it poses a direct threat to the immune system. This is because lymphatic cells are a type of white blood cell.

When functioning properly, these cells usually attack pathogens, bacteria, and viruses the moment they enter the body. Once these white blood cells are mutated through Lymphatic Cancers, the body begins to have difficulty fighting off even small viruses like mild colds.

This has the effect of turning mild conditions into severe medical crises.

Types of Cancer (1/2)
Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma
 Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma (Sometimes referred to as NHL) is the most common form of Lymphatic Cancers, with around 8 cases documented for every one cases of Hodgkin's Disease.

It is the 6th most common form of cancer in men, and the 5th most common cancer in women, thus posing a serious threat to everybody. Unfortunately, its prominence has been on the rise for decades.

Types of Cancer (2/2)
Hodgkin's Disease
 Hodgkin's Disease (sometimes referred to as HD) attacks the same area that Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma does: the lymphocytes. Hodgkin's Disease takes over these cells, and in doing so weakens the immune system.

This has the adverse effect of making the body more prone to sickness. Because of this, the symptoms of Hodgkin's Disease are very similar to Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma.

Medical Advancements (1/2)
Immunotherapy
 In its most basic form, immunotherapy is the practice of applying the immune system to combat the development and spread of cancerous cells.

While such therapies have existed for some time now, recent developments have made this form of treatment significantly more effective and comfortable for the patient when applied against Lymphatic Cancers.

Medical Advancements (2/2)
Chemotherapy
 Chemotherapy is the application of a powerful drug or combinations of drugs to combat cancerous cells in the body.

Although it is a treatment option that has historically been associated with negative side effects and inconsistent results, recent developments have sought to upset that understanding through the careful refinement of the practice.

How to Help (1/2)
Lymphoma Research Foundation
 The Lymphoma Research Foundation is an organization that has long since established itself as one of the most reputable authorities in regards to the research and development of new Lymphatic Cancer treatments.

They support the fight against Lymphatic Cancer on fronts both scientific and informational, and in doing so are able to actively engage the public in combating the disease.

How to Help (2/2)
Lymphoma Foundation of America
 The Lymphoma Foundation of America is an organization that has remained selflessly dedicated to the eradication of Lymphatic Cancers since its inception in 1986.

They aproach this goal on a variety of fronts to ensure that all avenues of progress can be considered. The foundation was started by Lymphoma patients, for Lymphoma patients.
