 Introduction (1/3)
test Cancers are diseases in which normal test cell development is disturbed by the unchecked growth of a varient on an ordinary test cell.

The testis are largely considered to be the most important element of the male reproductive system, as they contain the glands in which spermatoza (commonly known as sperm) and testosterone are created.

The two main types of Testicular Cancer are Seminomas and Nonseminoas, both of which are covered in great detail below.
Introduction (2/3)
It is an extremely rare form of cancer, occuring in less than half a percent of males in a given year.

Despite this, Testicular Cancer is currently the most common form of cancer found in men between the ages of 15 and 35. This fact has resulted in an estimated 195,000 men with a history of Testicular Cancer in the United States alone.

It is also one of the more treatable cancers, accounting for only a tenth of a percent of total cancer deaths.

Types of Cancer (1/2)
Seminomas
 Seminomas are one of the two primary forms of Testicular Cancer. A Seminoma is a form of cancer that begins in the cells that produces sperm.

Due to their place of origin, this form of Testicular Cancer typically is not extremely aggressive and as such is usually fairly treatable.

Seminomas develop and spread slowly, relative to Nonseminomas.

Types of Cancer (2/2)
Nonseminoas
 Nonseminomas are the other primary form of Testicular Cancer.

Although they are less common than Seminomas, they still pose a larger threat to most patients due to their ability to develop and spread relatively rapidly throughout the body.

Unlike Seminomas, they begin in the cells that form sperm or eggs.

Medical Advancements (1/2)
Self-Examinations
 The promotion of self-examination has been one of the most important factors in the early discovery of Testicular Cancer.

To understand this, one must first recognize the symptoms of Testicular Cancer. These can include, but are not limited to, things such as the enlargement of the testicle, a feeling of heaviness in the scrotum, a collection of fluid in the scrotum, and a persistent pain in the testicle or the scrotum.

Chemotherapy Improvements
 Unfortunately, many of the procedures that are currently feasable for treating Testicular Cancers have been known to produce uncomfortable side effects.

Perhaps the most common of these treatments is chemotherapy, which is used to great effect in many to subdue tumors that have spread beyond the point of origin.

 The Testicular Cancer Awareness Foundation is a nonprofit that strives to heighten the general public's awareness of the threat of Testicular Cancers.
